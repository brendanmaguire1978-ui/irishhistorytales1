Episode 12

The Gaelic Resistance: Donal Mór O’Brien, Donal Kavanagh & the Great Revolt

Published 17 June 2026

Episode overview

As Henry II faces rebellion across his empire, Gaelic rulers exploit Norman weakness in Ireland. Donal Mór O’Brien attacks Norman positions while Donal Kavanagh emerges as a major figure in the resistance.

This page traces the widening Gaelic resistance as events elsewhere in Henry II’s dominions created an opportunity to attack exposed Norman settlements and lordships in Ireland.

Topics covered

  • The effect of Henry II’s Great Revolt on Ireland
  • Donal Mór O’Brien’s attacks on Norman positions
  • Donal Kavanagh and resistance in Leinster
  • The growing vulnerability of the Norman colony
